Research Highlights

       1.       Functional nanoparticle materials — through molecular design and synthesis we have synthesized many

               functional nanoparticles that can be self-assembled into ordered structures for device applications.

        ●  Au nanoparticles (~12 nm) have been assembled with sulfur containing C60 (~2 nm) on gold electrodes with a

            15nm spacing to fabricate into single electron transistor and memory cells. (Langmuir 2002, Appl.Phys.Lett

            2002,US Patent  6586787)

        ●  SiO2 nanoparticles (~10nm) have been spin coated onto silicon substrate and fabricated into large area IR

            light emitting diodes, near lasing phenomena has been observed. (Optics Letters 2002, MRS Bulletin 2002,

            US Patent  filed)

        ●   Hydrogenated SiO2 nanoparticles emit IR (J. Mat. Res. 2002).

        ●  II-VI semiconductors such as CdS nanoparticles (5nm) have been spin coated onto different substrates and

             fabricated into flexible green light LED; the light intensity and emission spectra can be tuned by oxide

             impurities. (MRS  proceeding 2002, Jpn. J. Appl. Phy. 2003, US Patent granted 2004)

       2.       High performance organic-inorganic hybrids—through nanosizing inorganic materials that can be well

               dispersed in organic matrixes to form functional nanocomposites with outstanding optical, electrical, thermal and

               mechanical  properties.

         ●  TiO2, BiTiOx, BiZrOx nanoparticles have been in-situ forming and well dispersed in polyacrylate matrixes that

             exhibit  refractive indexes, high optical transparency for optical lenses, optical waveguide, optical adhesive,

             etc.(J. Mat. Res. 2004, US Patent 6492540)

        ●  SiO2 nanoparticles have been dispersed in epoxy matrix to form low shrinkage, high strength, biocompatible

             visible  light curable nanocomposites suitable for dental restorative material applications. (Biomaterials 2004,

             US Patent  filed)  

       3.        High K electronic ceramics — through chemical synthesis or sol-gel method, high purity and low sintering

                temperature ectronic ceramic thin films and powders have been prepared for chip capacitors, microwave filters,

                actuators and  nonvolatile memory.

        ●  High dielectric constant and low sintering temperature bismuth based microwave materials (J. Eur. Ceramic

            Society 2003)

        ●   High dielectric constant and ferroelectric bismuth titanate prepared by sol-gel method. (Mat. Chem. Phys.

             2003)

        ●   High dielectric constant and high quality factor barium based microwave dielectric material prepared by

             chemical methods. (MMA 2002)

        4.      Liquid crystalline epoxy resin — through molecular design, biphenol, azomethine mesogen groups have

                 been incorporated into the epoxy mainchain to form liquid crystalline epoxy resin with exceptional electrical,

                 thermal and  mechanical properties and low processing viscosity. They also exhibit low flammability. They are

                 good candidate materials to replace bromine and phosphorus based materials. (Polymer Degradation and

                 Stability 2004, J. Appl. Polym. Sci. 2000,ROC Taiwan Patent 120638)
